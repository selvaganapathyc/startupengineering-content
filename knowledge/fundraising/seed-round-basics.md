# Seed Round Basics

The "seed round" has drifted. It used to mean $500k from friends-and-family. Today it often means $2–5M from institutional investors with term sheets that rhyme with Series A. Knowing what you're actually signing up for matters.

## The shape of a modern seed

- **Round size**: $1M–$5M typical; up to $10M for strong AI or repeat founders.
- **Instrument**: usually SAFEs (post-money), sometimes a priced round.
- **Valuation cap**: $10M–$30M for most; higher in hot sectors.
- **Dilution**: 15–25% if priced; varies with SAFE stacking.
- **Board**: usually no board at SAFE stage; 3-person board if priced (2 founders + 1 investor).

Numbers shift with market cycles. Don't anchor on absolutes.

## SAFE vs priced round

**SAFE** (Simple Agreement for Future Equity): a convertible instrument. No valuation set today, converts at the next priced round. Fast, cheap (~$0 in legal), flexible.

**Priced round**: actual equity today. Valuation set, shares issued, board created. Slower, more legal ($25–75k), more formal.

Default for seed: SAFEs. Move to priced when you're raising enough ($3M+) that investors want clarity.

## Key terms to understand

- **Valuation cap** (SAFE) — the max valuation at which your SAFE converts. Lower is better for the investor.
- **Discount** — SAFE converts at a % discount to the next priced round's price. Stack on top of cap.
- **Pro-rata** — right for an investor to maintain their % in future rounds. Standard.
- **MFN** — "most favored nation." If you give later SAFE investors better terms, earlier ones get them too. Standard.
- **Liquidation preference** — investor gets their $ back before founders in an exit. 1× non-participating is standard; avoid anything else.

## What investors actually look for at seed

1. **Team.** Can you build it? Can you sell it? Why you?
2. **Market.** Is the prize big enough if you win?
3. **Insight.** Why now? Why is this a business now that wasn't two years ago?
4. **Traction.** Any signal of pull. Not required, but it shortens the conversation by weeks.

In that order. Deck doesn't matter much. Story matters a lot.

## Common traps

- **Raising too much.** Dilution compounds. $3M at $15M post is often better than $5M at $15M post.
- **Raising too little.** 12 months of runway is scary; 18–24 is safer.
- **Anchoring on valuation.** Terms matter more than the headline number.
- **Bad cap-table math.** Option pool expansions (pre- vs post-money) can silently cost you 5–10%.
- **Chasing famous investors.** A fund that'll answer your texts in 2 years is worth more than a logo that won't.
- **Negotiating against yourself.** "We were thinking..." is how founders lose leverage.

## The fundraising process

1. Build a target list (~40 investors).
2. Warm intros only — cold emails rarely work at seed.
3. Schedule densely. Two weeks of back-to-back pitches beats three months of spread-out ones.
4. Get to a term sheet; use it to close others.
5. Sign, close, get back to work.

## Rule of thumb

**Raise the minimum you need to reach the next milestone that unlocks the next round.** More than that is dilution you'll regret. Less is stress you don't need.
