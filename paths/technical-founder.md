---
title: Technical founder, zero to launch
audience: Solo technical founder
length: 7 skills
steps:
  - skill: define/idea-refine
    why: Before you code anything, make sure the idea survives contact with reality.
  - skill: discover/spec-driven-development
    why: Write the spec before the code — it's the cheapest way to change your mind.
  - skill: design/api-and-interface-design
    why: The shape of your first API decides the shape of the next year.
  - skill: develop/incremental-implementation
    why: Ship vertical slices. A working thin feature beats half a broken big one.
  - skill: develop/test-driven-development
    why: A handful of tests you trust will save you from the 2am regression.
  - skill: demonstrate/shipping-and-launch
    why: Launch isn't the end — it's when you find out what was wrong with your spec.
  - skill: deploy/ci-cd-and-automation
    why: Automate the deploy before you need to. Your future self has a runway deadline.
---

From "I have an idea" to "first paying customer" — the minimum engineering you
need, nothing more. This path is for one person doing everything: product,
architecture, code, and the scary conversations with reality. Skip the enterprise
habits you don't yet need. Do the seven things that compound.
