title: The scaling CTO
audience: Technical leader, seed to Series A
length: 9 skills
steps:
  - skill: design/api-and-interface-design
    why: The MVP's API is now a load-bearing wall. Redesign it deliberately, not accidentally.
  - skill: design/documentation-and-adrs
    why: Start writing down the "why" of decisions — future you and future hires will need it.
  - skill: develop/code-review-and-quality
    why: As the team grows, review is the main way your judgment scales.
  - skill: develop/code-simplification
    why: Complexity is the tax you pay on every future change. Cut it deliberately.
  - skill: demonstrate/performance-optimization
    why: The MVP worked at 200 users. Find out where it breaks at 20,000 before your customers do.
  - skill: deploy/security-and-hardening
    why: Your first real security conversation with a customer is on their timeline, not yours. Be ready.
  - skill: deploy/ci-cd-and-automation
    why: If a deploy is scary, you'll ship less of them. Make it boring.
  - skill: deploy/deprecation-and-migration
    why: You'll outgrow your first schema. The migration is survivable if you plan it.
  - skill: deploy/git-workflow-and-versioning
    why: A branching model that fit 2 engineers will grind when you're 12. Upgrade before the pain.
---

You shipped the MVP. Now it's breaking in ways the MVP never could. This path is
the decisions that get you from seed to Series A without a rewrite — the ones
where "we'll fix it later" is the wrong answer and "let's redo it properly" is
the wrong answer too.
